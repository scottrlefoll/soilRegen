Organic and Regenerative Agriculture

Soil Health

Regenerative farming is a holistic land management practice that focuses on improving soil health. The goals are to restore the soil's ability to capture water, carbon, and other nutrients, thus improving biodiversity, enhancing ecosystems, and combating climate change. This approach goes beyond sustainable agriculture by aiming not just to maintain, but to enhance soil fertility and global biodiversity.

Regenerative Farming

The principles of regenerative and organic farming include:

  •   --Soil Health Improvement: Maintaining soil health through practices like cover cropping and crop rotation.
  •   --Carbon Sequestration: Healthy soil creates, absorbs and stores more nutrients.
  •   --Biodiversity Enhancement: Fostering various plant species and supporting wildlife populations.
  •   --No-Tillage: Avoid soil disturbance, which destroys soil structure and reduces beneficial soil microbiology.
  •   --Integration of Livestock: Animals contribute to soil health by cycling nutrients and stimulating plant growth.
  •   --Perennial Crops: Plants that live longer and contribute to soil health by providing continual ground cover.

By prioritizing soil health, regenerative farming has the potential to create resilient farms that produce high-quality, nutrient-dense food, while also playing a crucial role in biodiversity and maintaining a healthy environment.
